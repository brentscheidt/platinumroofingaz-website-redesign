Flat & foam roofing

Flat and foam roofing in Phoenix — TPO, spray foam, and low-slope solutions.

Low-slope and flat roofs are everywhere in Arizona — on commercial buildings, patio covers, additions, and modern homes. We install, repair, and recoat TPO, EPDM, modified bitumen, and spray polyurethane foam systems.

When you need this

Ponding water, membrane failure, and foam recoats

Flat roofs in Arizona face unique challenges — standing water after monsoons, UV breakdown of membranes, and thermal expansion stress. If you're seeing ponding, bubbling, or cracks in your flat roof system, it's time for an inspection before the damage spreads.

Systems we work with

TPO, EPDM, modified bitumen, and spray foam

Each flat roof system has different strengths. TPO is energy-efficient and weldable. EPDM is durable and cost-effective. Modified bitumen handles foot traffic well. Spray polyurethane foam (SPF) provides insulation and seamless coverage. We'll recommend the right system for your building and budget.

Arizona context

Drainage, UV protection, and energy efficiency

Flat roofs in Arizona must handle sudden monsoon downpours and intense UV exposure year-round. Proper drainage slope, UV-resistant coatings, and reflective surfaces aren't optional — they're essential. We design every flat roof system with Arizona's climate as the starting point.

Lifespan How long does a flat roof last in Arizona?

It depends on the system. TPO and EPDM typically last 15-25 years. Spray foam with proper recoating can last indefinitely — each recoat extends the life another 10-15 years. Modified bitumen averages 15-20 years. Arizona's UV accelerates wear on all systems.

Foam recoat When does spray foam need to be recoated?

Spray foam roofs need recoating every 10-15 years to protect the foam from UV degradation. If you're seeing exposed foam, discoloration, or small cracks, it's time. Recoating is significantly cheaper than replacing the entire system.

Ponding Is standing water on my flat roof a problem?

Yes. Water that sits for more than 48 hours after rain accelerates membrane breakdown and adds weight stress. We can improve drainage with tapered insulation, scuppers, or internal drains to eliminate ponding areas.

Commercial Do you work on commercial flat roofs?

Yes. Most of our flat roof work is commercial — office buildings, retail centers, warehouses, and multi-unit properties. We coordinate with property managers to minimize tenant disruption and work around business hours when needed.
