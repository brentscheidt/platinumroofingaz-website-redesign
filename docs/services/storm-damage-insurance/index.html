Storm damage and insurance

Storm damage and insurance claims — we handle the process.

After a monsoon or hail event, most homeowners don't know where to start. Platinum Roofing inspects your roof, documents the damage properly, and works with your insurance adjuster so you don't have to figure it out on your own.

What we handle

Monsoon and hail damage assessment, emergency tarps, and insurance documentation

Arizona monsoon season runs June through September and brings wind, hail, and sudden water intrusion that can compromise a roof in one storm. Platinum responds quickly — we tarp exposed areas to prevent interior damage, conduct a thorough inspection, and produce the photo documentation and damage report your insurance company needs to process a claim accurately.

Signs of storm damage

Dented metal, cracked tile, granule loss, and water inside after a storm

Hail damage isn't always visible from the ground. Impact craters on metal flashing, cracked or displaced tiles, granule loss concentrated in impact patterns, and any new interior water staining after a storm are the signs we look for. If you're not sure whether your roof took damage in a recent event, an inspection is the only reliable way to know — and it's the first thing your insurer will require anyway.

What to expect

A documented inspection, adjuster coordination, and repairs completed correctly

We work alongside your adjuster — not against them. Our inspections are thorough and our documentation is clear because that's what gets claims approved and repairs completed to the right scope. If your roof needs emergency stabilization while the claim is processed, we handle that too. You'll know what's happening at every step.

After a stormWhat should I do right after a storm damages my roof?

Don't wait. If you see interior water staining or visible damage, call us immediately. We can tarp exposed areas to prevent further interior damage while the inspection and claim process gets started. The sooner damage is documented after the event, the stronger the claim.

CoverageWill my insurance cover the repair?

Storm damage from hail, wind, and monsoon events is covered under most standard homeowner policies. Your deductible applies, and some older policies have separate wind or hail deductibles. We'll help you understand what your policy covers before you file so there are no surprises.

Claim timingDo I need to file a claim before getting an inspection?

No. An inspection comes first. You need to know the scope of the damage before you file — and your insurer will send their own adjuster anyway. Having a professional inspection report before the adjuster arrives strengthens your position and ensures nothing gets missed in their assessment.

Filing deadlinesHow long do I have to file a storm damage claim?

Most Arizona homeowner policies require claims to be filed within one year of the damage event, but some carriers have tighter windows. Don't assume you have time. If you think your roof took damage in a storm — even months ago — contact us for an inspection before the filing window closes.
