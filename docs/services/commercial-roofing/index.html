Commercial roofing

Commercial roofing in Phoenix — built for business.

Flat roofs, tight timelines, and tenants still on-site. We scope commercial roofing around how your property actually operates — so work gets done without shutting you down.

What we install and repair

TPO, EPDM, and modified bitumen systems

We work with the full range of commercial flat roofing systems — TPO membrane for energy efficiency, EPDM for long-term flexibility, and modified bitumen for layered protection. Every system is selected based on your building's footprint, drainage profile, and sun exposure. We also handle commercial roof inspections, drain assessments, and proactive maintenance programs before small issues become major repairs.

Signs you need commercial roofing service

Ponding water, interior leaks, or a roof past its warranty

Standing water after rain is the most common early warning sign on Phoenix flat roofs. Other indicators: bubbling or cracked membrane, staining on interior ceilings, HVAC units with water intrusion around the curb, or a roof more than 15 years old with no documented maintenance history. If your building is under a lease or you manage multiple tenants, a documented inspection is worth doing before any issue becomes a liability.

SystemsWhat types of commercial roofs do you work on?

We install and repair TPO, EPDM, and modified bitumen flat roof systems. We also work on built-up roofing (BUR) and handle tapered insulation for drainage correction. If your building has a low-slope or flat roof, we can assess it regardless of what's currently on it.

OperationsHow do you minimize business disruption?

We plan access routes before the job starts, schedule loud or disruptive phases during off-hours when possible, and keep a dedicated point of contact for property managers throughout the project. Most commercial repairs and even full replacements can be phased so your building stays operational the entire time.

Multi-tenantDo you handle multi-tenant properties?

Yes. We work with property managers, HOAs, and asset managers on multi-tenant buildings and mixed-use properties. We're used to coordinating around lease terms, occupied units, and shared spaces. If you manage multiple buildings, we can set up a recurring inspection and maintenance schedule across your portfolio.

MaintenanceWhat maintenance does a commercial roof need?

Most commercial flat roofs should be inspected twice a year — once in spring before monsoon season and again in fall. Key maintenance tasks include clearing drains, checking membrane seams and flashing, inspecting HVAC curb penetrations, and documenting any areas showing wear. A maintained roof can last 20–30 years. A neglected one rarely makes it past 10.
