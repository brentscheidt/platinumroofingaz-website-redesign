Roof inspection

Roof inspection in Phoenix — know exactly what you're dealing with.

Before you spend money on repairs or replacement, get an honest assessment of what your roof actually needs. We inspect, document, and give you a clear picture — no upsell pressure, no guessing.

What a roof inspection covers

Pre-purchase inspections, annual checks, storm damage, and insurance documentation

A Platinum roof inspection covers the full system: shingles or membrane condition, flashing and sealant integrity, gutters and drainage, decking for soft spots or rot, and any penetration points around vents, skylights, and HVAC equipment. We also perform pre-purchase inspections for homebuyers who want an independent assessment before closing, annual maintenance inspections to catch wear before it becomes a leak, and post-storm assessments with detailed photo documentation that supports insurance claims. You get a written report every time.

Signs you should schedule an inspection now

Recent storm, active leak, visible damage, or a roof over 10 years old

You don't need an obvious problem to benefit from an inspection. If your roof is more than 10 years old and hasn't been professionally assessed, you're likely carrying undetected wear. Other reasons to schedule now: you saw hail or high winds in your area, you have water staining on interior ceilings or walls, you're buying or selling a home, your insurance carrier requested documentation, or a neighbor nearby has had roof damage and you want to confirm your status. Early detection almost always costs less than deferred maintenance.

What to expect

A thorough walk, a written report, and an honest recommendation

One of our licensed roofers walks the full roof surface, checks all transition points, and photographs anything worth documenting. The inspection typically takes 45 to 90 minutes depending on roof size and complexity. You get a written report that includes condition notes, photos of any problem areas, and a clear recommendation — whether that's no action needed, a targeted repair, or a full replacement evaluation. If your situation involves insurance, we document in the format carriers expect. We don't pad scopes or manufacture urgency.

FrequencyHow often should I have my roof inspected?

Once a year is the standard recommendation for most homes in the Phoenix metro. We suggest scheduling in spring — before monsoon season arrives — so any wear or vulnerability is addressed before heavy rain and wind. If you've had a significant storm with hail or 60+ mph winds, schedule an inspection within a few days, even if you don't see obvious damage from the ground.

What's includedWhat does a roof inspection include?

Our inspection covers the full roof system: surface material condition (shingles, tile, or membrane), flashing at all transition points, ridge and hip caps, valley drainage, gutters and downspouts, soffit and fascia, visible decking condition, and all penetration points. We photograph every area of concern and note approximate age of materials where visible. You receive a written report with findings and a clear next-step recommendation.

Home purchaseDo I need an inspection before buying a home?

Yes — and we strongly recommend a dedicated roofing inspection in addition to your general home inspection. General inspectors cover a wide scope and often flag roofing as something to "monitor" without specifying what it will cost or how soon it needs to be addressed. A roofing-specific inspection gives you a real cost estimate and a clearer picture of what you're taking on. In Arizona's climate, a failing roof can mean a $15,000–$30,000 decision you didn't budget for.

PricingIs a roof inspection free?

We offer free inspections for homeowners considering repair or replacement with Platinum. If you need a standalone inspection for a home purchase, estate, or insurance documentation with no scope of work attached, there may be a fee — we'll let you know upfront when you schedule. Either way, you get a written report and honest findings, not a sales pitch.

How it works

Our roof inspection process

  1. Schedule — Call us or submit a contact form. We'll confirm a time that works for you, typically within a few business days. Let us know if it's storm-related or time-sensitive and we'll prioritize accordingly.
  2. Inspect — A licensed roofer walks the full roof, checks all critical areas, and photographs every point of concern. We cover surface condition, drainage, flashings, penetrations, and structural indicators.
  3. Document — You receive a written report with photos, condition notes, and a clear assessment of urgency. If the inspection is for insurance purposes, we provide documentation in the format your carrier needs.
  4. Recommend — We tell you exactly what we found and what we'd recommend — repair, replacement, or nothing right now. No pressure. If you decide to move forward with us, the inspection informs the scope so there are no surprises.
