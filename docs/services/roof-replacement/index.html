Roof replacement

Roof replacement in Phoenix — when repair isn't enough.

When a roof has reached the end of its reliable life, another patch isn't the answer. Platinum Roofing replaces roofs completely — full tear-off, new underlayment, and a material system built to handle Arizona heat, UV exposure, and monsoon season.

What roof replacement involves

Full tear-off, new underlayment, and the right material for Arizona

A complete roof replacement means removing everything down to the deck, inspecting the structure underneath, and installing a new roofing system from the substrate up. We work with tile (concrete and clay), asphalt shingle, standing-seam metal, and flat roofing — and we'll help you choose the material that fits your home, your budget, and how long you plan to stay in the property.

Signs you may need a replacement

Age, recurring failures, and underlayment that can no longer protect

Arizona's heat and UV load accelerate roof aging faster than most of the country. If your roof is 20 or more years old, if you've had repeated leaks in different areas, or if an inspection finds the underlayment has deteriorated, repair becomes a short-term fix on a system that needs a full reset. Insurance-related total losses after major storm events also fall into this category.

Knowing when to replaceHow do I know if I need a new roof?

If your roof is approaching 20 years, if you're seeing leaks in multiple locations, or if an inspection finds the underlayment has failed, a replacement is almost always the stronger long-term move. We'll give you an honest assessment — including whether repair is still a reasonable option.

TimelineHow long does a roof replacement take?

Most residential replacements are completed in one to three days depending on the size of the home and the material being installed. Tile roofs take longer than shingle. We'll give you a specific timeline in the proposal so you can plan accordingly.

Materials in ArizonaWhat materials work best in Arizona?

Concrete tile and clay tile are the most common in the Phoenix metro — they handle UV and heat well and last 30–50 years with proper maintenance. Architectural shingle is a cost-effective option with a 20–30 year lifespan. Standing-seam metal is the premium choice for longevity and energy efficiency. We'll match the material to your home style, budget, and how long you want the roof to perform.

InsuranceDoes insurance cover roof replacement?

If the replacement is driven by storm damage — hail, wind, monsoon impact — your homeowner's policy may cover most or all of the cost. Age-related wear is generally not covered. If you've had storm damage, Platinum will document the loss properly and work with your adjuster to support the claim.
