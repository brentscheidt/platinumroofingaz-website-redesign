Common questions

What homeowners ask before choosing a roofer.

Repair vs. replace How do I know if my roof needs repair or replacement?

It depends on the roof's age, the extent of damage, and the condition of the underlayment. A 10-year-old roof with a single leak is usually a repair. A 25-year-old roof with multiple failure points is usually a replacement. We'll tell you which one makes sense after the inspection.

Cost How much does a new roof cost in Phoenix?

It varies widely based on roof size, pitch, material, and access. A shingle reroof on a typical Phoenix home ranges from $8,000-$15,000. Tile reroofs and metal systems cost more. We provide a clear, written estimate after the inspection — no pressure, no surprises.

Insurance Will my insurance pay for a new roof?

If the damage was caused by a covered event (storm, hail, wind), your homeowner's insurance typically covers the repair or replacement minus your deductible. We document the damage and work with your adjuster to support the claim.

Timeline How long does it take to replace a roof?

Most residential reroofs take 1-3 days once we start. Scheduling depends on the season and our workload. We'll give you a clear timeline before we begin and keep you updated throughout.
