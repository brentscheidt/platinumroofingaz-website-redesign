Roof repair

Roof repair in Phoenix — find the leak, fix it right.

Whether it's a slow drip after monsoon season or visible tile damage from hail, Platinum Roofing tracks down the actual source of the problem and repairs it completely — not just the spot you can see.

What roof repair covers

Leaks, flashing failures, storm damage, and broken tile or shingles

Most Arizona roof problems don't require a full replacement. A properly diagnosed repair — flashing re-sealed, cracked tiles replaced, storm-damaged shingles swapped out — can add years to a roof that still has life left in it. We work with tile, shingle, and flat roofing systems across the Phoenix metro.

Signs you may need a repair

Water stains, missing material, or damage after a storm

Interior water stains or wet drywall after rain are the most common signs. Others include cracked, slipped, or missing tiles, lifted or curling shingles, deteriorated flashing around chimneys or vents, and visible granule loss on asphalt. If you're not sure what you're looking at, a professional inspection is always the right first move.

What to expect

A clear diagnosis before any work is quoted or started

We don't patch symptoms. Every repair starts with a full inspection so we can identify the actual failure point — not just treat what's visible. You'll know what the problem is, what the repair involves, and whether repair is the right long-term answer or whether the roof has reached the point where replacement makes more sense.

Repair vs replacementHow do I know if my roof can be repaired?

Most roofs under 15–18 years old with a single point of failure are good repair candidates. If the underlayment is deteriorating, multiple areas are failing, or the roof has been patched repeatedly without lasting results, replacement may be the stronger investment. We'll tell you honestly which applies to your roof.

TimelineHow long does a roof repair take?

Most residential repairs are completed in a single day, often in a few hours. Larger repairs involving multiple sections or significant flashing work may extend to a full day. We'll give you a clear timeline when we scope the job.

InsuranceDoes insurance cover roof repairs?

It depends on the cause. Sudden damage from storms, hail, or wind is generally covered under most homeowner policies. Wear-and-tear or deferred maintenance typically is not. If your damage followed a storm event, Platinum can document the damage and work through the claim process with you.

Knowing when to replaceWhen is it time to replace instead of repair?

When repairs are recurring on the same sections, when the roof is approaching or past its rated lifespan, or when the cost of repair approaches the cost of replacement — that's when a full system reset becomes the more practical choice. We'll walk you through the comparison so you can make a confident decision.
