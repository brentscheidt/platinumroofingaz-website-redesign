## Executive Summary

platinumroofingaz.com is a very small, Squarespace-based marketing site with a handful of core pages (Home, About, Services, Blog, Contact) and a short blog archive (at least three posts dated mid-late 2025).

The current footprint is **not structurally built to win local, high-intent search terms** (e.g., "roof repair Phoenix") because it lacks dedicated service pages, service-area/city landing pages, proof assets (projects/case studies), and strong internal linking pathways that funnel visitors to conversion actions.

### Urgent Credibility Issues
- **Conflicting phone numbers** across key pages (homepage shows one number, About/Blog show a different number)
- **Blog post contains placeholder text** ("[Your Company Name]") and a formatting artifact ("H2: ...") — reads as low-quality/unedited template content

### Social Audit Constraints
- Social auditing was constrained — could not reliably fetch Meta platforms for follower counts
- Website links to Instagram but no Facebook link was visible in the site extraction
- Social-channel linking may be incomplete or inconsistent

### Competitive Landscape
Multiple Phoenix-market roofing sites demonstrate deeper content architecture (service pages, city pages, FAQs with pricing guidance, conversion-focused CTAs). This points to a straightforward path to outperform: rebuild the information architecture for local intent, standardize trust signals, and publish a sustained content program.

## Site Crawl and Technical SEO Findings

### Platform and Hosting
- Site runs on **Squarespace** (images.squarespace-cdn.com)
- Platform constraints affect: technical SEO controls, canonical handling, sitemap generation, script loading patterns, page-speed tuning, third-party scripts, template-level changes

### URL Structure
Observed core URLs:
- `/` (Home)
- `/about`
- `/services`
- `/blog` and `/blog/...`
- `/contact`

**Gap:** No city/service-area pages (e.g., `/phoenix-az/roof-repair` or `/service-areas/phoenix`). Competitors publish many location pages and supporting clusters.

### Titles, Headings, and On-Page Content
- Homepage presents a brand headline and primary contact block
- About page "Who we are" is keyword-heavy and written for "Arizona roofing" relevance
- Services page is a **single page** with three high-level sections (Roof Repairs, New construction, Commercial Roofing) — NOT dedicated service pages
- Blog index shows three posts attributed to Tyler Brown

### Content Quality Issues (Urgent)
- Monsoon post includes **"[Your Company Name]"** placeholder
- Same post includes "H2: 5. Reinforce Roof Edges and Valleys" as literal text (formatting artifact)

### NAP Consistency (Urgent)
- Home page shows **(602)-435-3544**
- About page shows **(480)-974-1595**
- Blog index/posts display **(480)-974-1595**
- NAP inconsistency affects user trust and business-entity consistency across listings

### Images and Alt Text
- Many images have descriptive alt text (e.g., "Two workers in neon yellow shirts...")
- Alt text reads like generic stock-photo descriptions rather than brand/service descriptors
- Optimizing alt text should be secondary to building the architecture

### Structured Data and Schema
- Could not verify actual schema payloads (JSON-LD on Home/About/Contact)
- Priority schema types: `Organization` (logo, address, phone, sameAs)
- Most specific subtype: `LocalBusiness` > `RoofingContractor` on Schema.org with strong address/geo fields and consistent NAP

### Technical Elements (Needs Verification)
- Robots.txt, sitemap, canonical tags, Core Web Vitals could not be validated directly
- Recommended: confirm robots.txt allows crawling, confirm sitemap contains all key pages
- Use PageSpeed Insights for both lab (Lighthouse) and field (CrUX) data including LCP, CLS, INP

## Social Channel Audit for Facebook and Instagram

### What Could Be Verified
- Site includes Instagram link ("Follow us on social" -> Instagram)
- No Facebook link visible in site crawl — either not linked or not captured

### What Could Not Be Verified
- Follower counts, post cadence, engagement rate, content-type mix — access/caching errors
- Use platform-native analytics: Instagram Insights, Facebook Page Insights

### Actionable Social Strategy (Regardless of Follower Count)
Content pillars for "proof + proximity + process":
- **Before/after carousels** with 1-sentence scope + city mention
- **Short "storm response" reels** during monsoon season (inspection -> tarp -> repair)
- **30-45 second "roofing myths" series** (insurance, warranties, foam recoats, tile underlayment)
- **Crew/process content** (safety, cleanup, timeline) to reduce homeowner anxiety

CTA hygiene (every post maps to one): "Call now for a free inspection" / "Request quote" / "DM 'INSPECT' for scheduling" / "Check our reviews"

### AI-Driven Content Automation
- **Field capture:** crew uploads photos/videos to shared folder with a one-line note
- **Caption generation:** AI prompt template generates caption + 5 local hashtags + 1 CTA + alt-text draft
- **Brand compliance:** auto-check for banned claims (pricing guarantees, insurance promises)
- **Scheduling:** queue content into weekly calendar (3 posts/week + 2 stories/week baseline)

## Keyword and Content Opportunity Analysis

### Current Implicit Targeting
- About page uses language aligned with "roof repair", "roof replacement", "roof installation"
- Services are mentioned but without dedicated pages
- Blog topics: Arizona monsoons, leak prevention, roofing materials for AZ homes

### Structural Keyword Gap
The most expensive and valuable roofing keywords are **local, transactional, and service-specific**. The current architecture makes it hard to:
- Rank a single "Services" page for multiple distinct intents ("foam roofing Phoenix", "tile roof repair Phoenix", "commercial TPO repair Phoenix")
- Build topical authority around each service line

### Prioritized Target Keywords

**High-intent core services:**
- Roof repair Phoenix AZ
- Roof replacement Phoenix AZ
- Roofing company Phoenix AZ
- Commercial roofing Phoenix AZ
- Emergency roof repair Phoenix
- Roof inspection Phoenix

**Material/system specific:**
- Foam roofing Phoenix
- Tile roof repair Phoenix
- Metal roofing Phoenix
- Flat roof repair Phoenix
- TPO roof repair Phoenix

**Storm/insurance intent:**
- Storm damage roof repair Phoenix
- Roof leak repair Phoenix
- Monsoon roof leak repair Phoenix
- Hail damage roof inspection Phoenix

**Near-me modifiers:**
- Roof repair near me
- Roofing contractor near me

### Recommended Content Architecture

A conversion-first SEO architecture:
- **Pillar:** "Roofing Services in Phoenix, AZ" (indexable, city-targeted)
- **Cluster service pages** (each 800-1,600 words + FAQs + proof):
  - Roof Repair
  - Roof Replacement
  - Commercial Roofing
  - Foam Roofing / Flat Roofing
  - Tile Roofing
  - Metal Roofing
  - Storm Damage / Insurance Support
- **Cluster city pages** (unique copy + projects + local proof):
  - Phoenix, Scottsdale, Mesa, Tempe, Glendale, Peoria, Chandler, Gilbert

### On-Page Templates

**Service page title:** `{{Service}} in {{City}}, AZ | Free Inspection | Platinum Roofing Group`

**Service page meta description:** `Need {{service}} in {{city}}? Licensed & insured roofing team. Fast scheduling, clear estimates, and long-lasting repairs. Call for a free inspection.`

**City page H1:** `Roof Repair & Replacement in {{City}}, AZ`

**FAQ pattern (every money page):** Cost range (with caveats), Timeline, Materials/compatibility, Warranty/guarantee, Insurance/storm support (if applicable)

### Backlinks and Authority-Building
High-leverage sources:
- Suppliers/manufacturers partner pages (if certified installers)
- Local chambers, business associations
- Local sponsorships (youth sports, community events) with a linked sponsor page
- Local news features (storm damage response, safety tips)

### Review Strategy with Risk Controls
- After job completion: text/email with a single review link from the CRM
- If customer reports an issue: route to internal resolution first
- Capture review snippets on-site only if allowed/accurate
- Avoid "review schema" misuse unless you meet Google's guidelines
