Site purpose and audience

What the site does

This site is a lead-generation and trust-building platform for Platinum Roofing Group. Its primary job is to convert local search traffic into phone calls, form submissions, and scheduled inspections.

Who it serves

  • Homeowners — searching for roof repair, replacement, or inspection in the Phoenix metro
  • Property managers — managing commercial or multi-unit roofing needs
  • Insurance claimants — looking for help after storm damage
  • Home buyers — needing pre-purchase roof inspections

What makes it better than competitors

Most Phoenix roofing sites are either (a) generic template sites with one "Services" page, or (b) bloated sites with thin, keyword-stuffed content. This site has dedicated pages for each service AND each city, with real content that answers specific questions. That's the structure Google rewards for local search.

Homepage — hero, trust stack, and conversion flow

Why the hero works

The hero leads with "Phoenix roofing done right" and two CTAs (free inspection + phone number). The drone video adds visual proof without slowing load time (it's a muted autoplay, not a YouTube embed). Visitors see what they need within 2 seconds: who you are, what you do, and how to contact you.

Trust stack

The 4-badge trust strip ("Since 2010 | Licensed & Insured | Free Inspections | Residential & Commercial") appears above the fold on mobile. Competitors like Arizona's Finest Roofing and Star Roofing use similar patterns — homeowners expect these signals immediately.

Process section

The 4-step "How it works" section (Call → Inspect → Scope → Execute) reduces anxiety. Homeowners hiring a roofer for the first time don't know what to expect. This tells them exactly what happens after they call.

Review section

Testimonials near the bottom of the homepage provide social proof before the final CTA. These should be replaced with real Google reviews once available.

Service pages — why 8 dedicated pages instead of 1

The SEO case

Google can't rank one generic "Services" page for "roof repair Phoenix" AND "metal roofing Phoenix" AND "commercial roofing Phoenix." Each search intent needs its own page with matching content, title, and FAQ. Your competitors who rank well (NextGen Coating, BUILT Roofing, Eco Roofing) all use dedicated service pages.

The 8 services

  • Roof Repair — targets leak, flashing, and storm repair searches
  • Roof Replacement — targets reroof and "new roof" searches
  • Commercial Roofing — targets flat roof, TPO, and commercial property searches
  • Metal Roofing — targets standing seam, energy-efficient, and premium roofing searches
  • Roof Inspection — targets free inspection, pre-purchase, and annual checkup searches
  • Storm & Insurance — targets monsoon, hail, insurance claim, and emergency searches
  • Tile Roofing — targets tile repair, underlayment, and AZ-specific tile searches
  • Flat & Foam — targets TPO, spray foam, flat roof, and foam recoat searches

Page structure

Every service page follows the same pattern: hero with service-specific headline → 3 content panels → 4-step process → city links → 4 FAQs → CTA. This consistency helps both users (they know where to find info) and search engines (structured content is easier to index).

City pages — local SEO for 8 Phoenix-metro markets

Why city pages matter

When someone in Mesa searches "roof repair Mesa AZ," Google looks for pages that specifically mention Mesa with relevant roofing content. Without a dedicated Mesa page, your site competes against competitors who have one. City pages are the single most effective local SEO tactic for service businesses.

What makes these different from "thin" city pages

Many roofing sites create city pages by swapping the city name in a template. Google detects and penalizes this. Each of your city pages has unique content about the local housing stock, common roof types, and specific climate factors for that area. Phoenix is different from Scottsdale is different from Peoria.

Blog — content strategy and SEO value

Why blog content matters

Blog articles capture informational searches — people who aren't ready to buy yet but are researching. "When to replace vs repair your roof" brings in homeowners who are thinking about their roof but haven't called anyone. The blog turns researchers into leads over time.

Current articles

  • When to replace vs. repair — captures "repair or replace roof" searches
  • Arizona monsoon roof prep — seasonal content that peaks before June
  • Filing a roof damage insurance claim — captures insurance-related searches

Content calendar recommendation

Publish 2-4 articles per month covering: seasonal prep (monsoon, winter), material comparisons, cost guides, insurance tips, and project spotlights. Each article should link to relevant service and city pages to strengthen internal linking.

Technical design — speed, mobile, and accessibility

No frameworks, no bloat

The site is built with hand-written HTML, CSS, and vanilla JavaScript. No React, no WordPress, no page builder. This means sub-second load times, zero JavaScript framework overhead, and full control over every element.

Mobile-first responsive design

Every page is tested at 375px (iPhone), 768px (tablet), and 1024px+ (desktop). The navigation collapses to a hamburger menu on mobile. CTAs are thumb-friendly. Text is readable without zooming.

Hosting

The site is hosted on Cloudflare Workers, one of the fastest CDN platforms in the world. Pages load from the edge server closest to the visitor. There's no origin server to be slow.

Accessibility

All images have alt text. Navigation is keyboard-accessible. The color contrast meets WCAG AA standards. Screen reader labels are present on interactive elements.

Tracking and analytics — how to measure performance

Current tracking

The site has built-in analytics events that fire on page views, CTA clicks, and review note submissions. These are sent to the GAIOS analytics endpoint for internal tracking.

Google Analytics setup (recommended)

Before launch, add Google Analytics 4 (GA4) with these events configured:

  • Page views by service and city page
  • Phone call clicks (tel: link tracking)
  • Contact form submissions
  • CTA button clicks by location on page

Google Search Console

Connect Search Console to monitor which keywords drive traffic, which pages are indexed, and any crawl issues. This is the most important tool for measuring SEO performance.

Key metrics to watch

  • Phone calls per week — the primary conversion metric
  • Form submissions per week — secondary conversion metric
  • Organic search impressions — visibility in Google results
  • Click-through rate — are people clicking your search results?
  • Page speed scores — use PageSpeed Insights monthly

Review system — how the feedback widget works

What it does

The "Send note" button on every page lets anyone submit feedback about the site. Notes include the page, section, text, and optional screenshots. They're stored in a Cloudflare D1 database and dispatched to AI for processing.

How to use it

Click "Send note" or "Feedback" on any page. Type what you want changed, optionally paste a screenshot, and hit send. Notes are queued for review and implementation.

Who sees the notes

Notes are visible to the GAIOS system (the AI team managing the site build). They are not public and are not visible to site visitors.
