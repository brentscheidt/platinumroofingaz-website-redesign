Mesa, AZ

Roofing in Mesa, AZ — serving Arizona's third-largest city.

Mesa covers a lot of ground — literally. From older neighborhoods near downtown to newer subdivisions out toward Gilbert, the city has roofs at every age and in every condition. We've worked throughout Mesa for over 15 years and know exactly what kinds of problems come up here and how to handle them.

Aging shingle and tile systems

A lot of Mesa roofs are at or past their expected lifespan

Mesa is Arizona's third-largest city with over 500,000 residents, and its housing stock reflects decades of growth waves — 1970s and 1980s subdivisions near downtown, 1990s master-planned communities in the central area, and 2000s–2010s development pushing east toward Gilbert. Homes built in the 1970s through 1990s are now carrying roofs that are 25 to 50 years old. Shingle roofs from that era were typically rated for 20–25 years. Tile systems can last longer, but the underlayment beneath the tile usually fails well before the tiles themselves crack or break. If your home was built before 2000 and the roof hasn't been replaced, it's worth finding out what condition it's actually in.

East Valley storm exposure

Mesa's east side takes some of the heaviest monsoon hits in the valley

The eastern portions of Mesa sit in one of the valley's most active hail and wind corridors — storm cells tracking in from the Superstition Mountains regularly drop hail on neighborhoods from Red Mountain to Eastmark before the rest of the valley even gets rain. High winds, heavy rain, and hail events are not unusual — and the damage they cause often doesn't show up until the next significant rainfall. If your home was hit by a storm and you're not sure whether the roof was damaged, we can inspect it and give you a clear, documented picture of what happened.

HOA-managed communities

Many Mesa neighborhoods have HOA roofing requirements

Mesa's master-planned communities and HOA-governed neighborhoods often have specifications for approved roofing materials and colors. We're experienced with HOA approval processes and can help you select materials that meet your community's standards. We provide the documentation your HOA needs so the approval process moves efficiently alongside the project itself.
