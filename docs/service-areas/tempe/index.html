Tempe, AZ

Roofing in Tempe, AZ — from college-town rentals to commercial properties.

Tempe's building mix is unlike anywhere else in the valley — older residential neighborhoods sit alongside ASU-adjacent rental properties, mid-century commercial buildings, and light industrial corridors near I-10. Whether you own a rental property, a small business, or a home near the water, Platinum Roofing has the experience to handle it.

Rental properties

Deferred maintenance catches up with rental roofs fast

Rental properties in Tempe — especially those near ASU — often go years without a professional roof inspection. With over 70,000 students enrolled at Arizona State University and a rental occupancy rate among the highest in the East Valley, a large portion of Tempe's residential stock turns over constantly without any owner-driven roof maintenance. By the time a tenant reports a leak, the underlying damage is usually more extensive than the visible stain on the ceiling suggests. We work with landlords and property managers to inspect, document, and repair or replace roofs on occupied properties with minimal disruption to tenants and rental income.

Commercial and flat roofs

Tempe's commercial corridor has a lot of flat and low-slope roofing

The commercial and light industrial properties along Tempe's I-10 and University Drive corridors are dominated by built-up, foam, and TPO flat roof systems. Tempe Town Lake's redevelopment has added a dense concentration of newer mixed-use and commercial buildings near the waterfront, while the older industrial corridor west of the airport carries a high volume of aging flat roofs due for major work. These roofs require regular maintenance to perform — ponding water, seam separation, and failed penetration flashings are the most common failure points. We service, repair, and replace flat and low-slope commercial roofs throughout Tempe and can work around business hours when needed.

Storm runoff and drainage

Tempe's smaller lots and dense neighborhoods make drainage problems worse

Tight lot spacing and older drainage infrastructure in Tempe's residential neighborhoods means storm runoff has fewer places to go. Clogged gutters, undersized scuppers, and improper slope on flat roofs can turn a heavy monsoon into a serious interior water event. We assess drainage as part of every inspection and can recommend cost-effective solutions that prevent the problem from repeating.
