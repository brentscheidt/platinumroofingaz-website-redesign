## What The Research Says

### Core business problem

The old Platinum site was too thin to compete for local, high-intent roofing searches in Phoenix and the surrounding market. It had a small page count, weak internal linking, no meaningful service-page depth, no city-page depth, and very little proof content.

### Urgent trust issues from the old site

- Phone numbers were inconsistent across key pages.
- At least one blog post contained placeholder/template text.
- The public site felt generic rather than premium or field-tested.

### What stronger competitors are doing

- Dedicated service pages instead of one generic services page.
- Dedicated city or service-area pages.
- More aggressive above-the-fold CTAs.
- Proof assets such as project examples, reviews, FAQs, and process framing.
- Insurance and storm-damage hubs.
- Better internal linking between service pages, location pages, and conversion pages.

### SEO opportunities the research prioritized

- `roof repair Phoenix AZ`
- `roof replacement Phoenix AZ`
- `roofing company Phoenix AZ`
- `commercial roofing Phoenix AZ`
- `emergency roof repair Phoenix`
- `roof inspection Phoenix`
- `foam roofing Phoenix`
- `tile roof repair Phoenix`
- `metal roofing Phoenix`
- `flat roof repair Phoenix`
- `TPO roof repair Phoenix`
- `storm damage roof repair Phoenix`
- `roof leak repair Phoenix`
- `monsoon roof leak repair Phoenix`

- Dedicated service pages, not just a summary services page

- A storm-damage and insurance-support content lane

## Positioning Direction

### Brand tone

Platinum should read as:

- Arizona-specific
- premium but not fake-luxury
- direct and practical
- inspection-first
- durable, clean, and detail-aware

Platinum should not read as:

- generic contractor brochure copy
- overhyped sales language
- commodity low-price roofing
- AI-slop service filler

### Core message

Platinum is the Arizona roofing company for clients who want a cleaner process, stronger judgment, and a better-looking finished result.

### Supporting message pillars

- Arizona experience since 2010
- residential and commercial capability
- climate-fit roofing decisions for heat, UV, drainage, and storm load
- consultation-first project scoping
- stronger visual finish standard than generic competitors

## Page-Level Content Requirements

### Home

- Strong Arizona-specific headline
- Fast trust stack near the top: years in market, service lanes, inspection CTA, phone, and proof
- Clear links into the top service pages
- Clear links into the top city pages
- A short process section
- A proof section with project or review snippets
- A strong CTA block before footer

### Service pages

Each service page should include:

- city-aware title and metadata pattern for live launch
- service-specific headline
- Arizona conditions context
- signs the customer needs this service
- repair vs replacement logic where relevant
- process breakdown
- proof or recent project block
- FAQs
- persistent CTA

### City pages

Each city page should include:

- unique city-specific intro
- services offered in that city
- local project or proof content
- relevant climate or housing-stock context when useful
- links to related service pages
- review or testimonial support
- strong call and contact CTA

### About

The About page should move from generic company story to trust-building proof:

- why Platinum exists
- how Platinum scopes work
- what quality means in practice
- Arizona context
- team/process credibility

### Contact

- single canonical phone number
- single canonical email
- short form
- clear response expectation
- service area summary
- optional inspection CTA language

## Design Direction

### Keep

- bold typography
- premium visual tone
- Arizona-specific atmosphere
- high-contrast layout
- clean CTA hierarchy

### Add in next pass

- more proof-driven sections, not just brand language
- stronger layout separation between residential, commercial, and storm lanes
- space for reviews and project evidence
- page templates that can scale across service and city pages

### Avoid

- generic roofing blue-and-white template aesthetics
- stock-photo dependence without real project proof
- overbuilt motion that hides content
- long hero copy without immediate trust signals

## SEO and Technical Requirements For Live Launch

These are not for the preview-only state. They are for the public launch build.

- remove `noindex` only when launch-ready
- implement canonical tags on all pages
- generate an XML sitemap with service pages, city pages, and blog pages
- add `Organization` and `RoofingContractor` schema
- validate one canonical NAP everywhere
- run PageSpeed Insights on core pages
- tighten internal linking across home, services, cities, projects, and contact

## Social and Content System

The research recommends a proof-based content engine. The site should support and reuse that system.

- before and after project posts
- storm-response content during monsoon season
- short roofing myth education content
- crew and process content that lowers homeowner anxiety
- reuse field-captured project media for site and social

## Decision For The Next Design Round

The next round should stop treating the site as a polished brochure and start treating it as a local-intent conversion system.

That means:

- fewer abstract brand statements
- more service specificity
- more city specificity
- more proof
- more conversion structure

The current testsite is a strong visual reset. The next pass needs to turn it into a stronger local SEO and lead-generation machine.
